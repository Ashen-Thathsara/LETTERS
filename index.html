Project Scope

Literature Survey

Looking at the current research in Sri Lanka, most of it focuses on attempts to identify children with ASD by recognizing facial expressions [1]. Using apps like Tobii Pro to capture facial expressions and analyze social interactions has been explored [2]. It seems that this eye tracking and facial expression recognition technology is adapted not to the school system but to the medical field.

Also, the “EarlySee” mobile app [3] uses facial expression recognition to assess responsiveness in children and “Volafriends” [4] recognizes emotion through facial landmark and head posture assessment, but they are often not culturally adapted and do not meet the specific needs of children in Sri Lanka.

PROPOSED SOLUTION

PROPOSED SOLUTION

Learning disabilities, which are common in children, receive very limited attention in the school systems of developing countries. There are limited detection and intervention applications using deep learning and machine learning. But these applications have not targeted the three cognitive abilities of speech, writing and color recognition of disabled children who are able to work in both Sinhala and English, which are the main languages of the Sri Lankan school system. This research will lead to the development of a mobile application which is aimed at the school system to develop the cognitive abilities of children with ASD and other neurodevelopmental disorders, aged 6 to 9 years. This research will lead to the development of a mobile application aimed at developing the cognitive abilities of children aged 6 to 9 years with ASD and other neurodevelopmental disorders. The primary objective of this application is to develop the speaking, writing and color recognition abilities of children with disabilities in the Sri Lankan school system, through the medium of both Sinhala and English as the main dual languages, and set a motivation to raise the educational standards.



METHODOLOGY

METHODOLOGY

The proposed system consists of five main functionalities,

  1. 1. Sinhala Voice Recognition
  2. 2. English Voice Recognition
  3. 3. Sinhala Handwriting Recognition
  4. 4. English Handwriting Recognition
  5. 5. Color Recognition

Using the mobile application, a user can provide his voice and handwriting as inputs in Sinhala and English, two of the main languages of Sri Lanka. First the user accesses the application and selects his language. He is then directed to each level. In the first level he is randomly shown a single simple word of two letters with dashes. He should write that word on the score line using his pen. Then a simple picture related to that word is shown with colors. After some time the image will be displayed without color. He should color the picture like the previous picture using the colors we give. He is then shown the word again. But this time it is not for writing as before, but for reading. After uttering that word loudly will show him the results of his overall progress. It also informs his level. The mobile application offers users the unique opportunity to engage with it using both voice and handwriting inputs, specifically in Sinhala and English, which are two predominant languages in Sri Lanka. Upon launching the application, users are prompted to select their preferred language. Once chosen, they are seamlessly guided through various levels of interaction.

At the outset, users encounter the initial level, where they are presented with a randomly selected simple word comprising two letters, depicted with dashes as placeholders. Utilizing a stylus or pen, users are tasked with writing the word on the designated score line within the application interface. Subsequently, a corresponding image, relevant to the word, is displayed with vibrant colors. However, after a brief period, the image transitions to grayscale, prompting users to recreate the original color scheme using the provided palette. Following this visual exercise, users are reintroduced to the word previously presented. This time, however, the objective shifts from writing to reading comprehension. Users are encouraged to vocalize the word aloud, triggering the application to record and analyze their pronunciation. Upon completion, the application provides immediate feedback on the user's overall performance, showcasing their progress and proficiency level.

This iterative process not only fosters language acquisition and comprehension but also offers users a dynamic and engaging learning experience. By incorporating multimodal inputs - voice and handwriting the application caters to diverse learning styles, promoting active participation and retention of language skills. Moreover, the progressive nature of the application, with its tiered levels and personalized feedback, ensures a tailored learning journey for each user. This approach fosters a sense of achievement and motivation, encouraging continued engagement and improvement over time.

SPECIFIC OBJECTIVE

Develop a mobile app that helps autistic children improve their English reading by
adapting teaching methods to their individual needs.

  • Measuring and developing children's ability to read Sinhala Words

    This objective includes developing mechanisms within the mobile application to identify and analyze the specific deficits a child with a speech disorder faces in reading.

  • Developing children's English Language reading skills and measuring progress

    The primary objective of this study is to improve the speech progress of children struggling with English speech disorders, with a particular focus on those with delays in English speech development.

  • Measuring and developing children's ability to writing English words

    To develop and evaluate a technology-driven educational intervention utilizing Histogram of Oriented Gradients (HOG) for English handwriting recognition

  • Developing child’s writing skill, Object and word recognition speed and color matching skill

    Assess the effectiveness of a mobile application designed to improve memory and color recognition skills in children with autism.
